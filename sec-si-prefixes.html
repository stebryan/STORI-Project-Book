    Section 2.3 Common Prefixes in the SI System

    Now that we have our base units we can talk about bigger and smaller units. All units in the SI system are related to the base unit by a power of 10.The power of 10 is indicated by a prefix that is added before the unit.For example, using this system, we can recognize that there are 1000 millimeters in a meter, where milli is the prefix meaning 1000. Similarly, you could also say that there is 0.001, or one thousandth of a meter in a millimeter.

    Insight 2.3.1. Using the SI System.

    The symbol for kilo is lowercase k. One kilo unit equals the base unit times 10 to the 3rd power. The "unit" of the base could be any of the SI units such as gram, meter, second, or mole. For instance, 1 kilometer equals 10 to the third power, \(10^{3} \text{,}\) or one thousand, meters. Compared to a meter, a kilometer is very long. It takes one thousand meters to make a kilometer. Letâ€™s also look at a very small prefix. The prefix nano uses the symbol lowercase n and one nano unit equals 10 to the negative 9 base units. You could also think of it this way, \(10^{9} \) nano units equals one base unit. For example, \(10^9\) nanoseconds equals one second, so compared to a second, a nanosecond is incredibly short.
