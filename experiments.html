    Section 1.6 Experiments

    Have you noticed that we have mentioned the word experiment quite a bit so far? We need experiments to test our hypotheses, so what is an experiment exactly? Experiments are very controlled procedures that are designed to collect data and observations that will support or nullify our hypothesis. Commonly, an experimenter will have a control group and an experimental group for testing. The control group is the experiment or trial that is fixed and used for comparison, while the experimental group will differ from the control groups by a specific influencing factor. It is also common to repeat experiments multiple times in order to ensure reproducible results.
